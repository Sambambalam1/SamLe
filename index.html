Team Profile

Team Name

Team Name: Genesis

The reason behind the name of the team being ‘Genesis,’ is that Genesis is often the word used to ascribe the ‘origin or mode of formation of something,’ essentially meaning that when consumers have found our company (typically through the use of our products, I would imagine), they have literally found the origin, the ‘genesis’ to it. However, it also takes on an alternate meaning. In the bible, Genesis chapter 11, there is the Tower of Babel, which was how Christianity explained the differing languages. The shortest and rather oversimplified version of the story is that, decades after the great flood, humanity attempted to build a tower so that they could reach heaven. God, annoyed at the antics of humanity, struck the tower down, and scattered humanity across the world (thus making different cultures), and cofounds their speech so that they can no longer understand each other, explaining the creation of languages. I think, considering the overall nature of our first project, it seemed a perfect fit for the name of our company; it can’t be ‘Babel’ because by limiting us to specifically languages then that would prevent us as a group pursuing any further in different areas, as well as bable being the origination of the word ‘babble,’ which translated means ‘to talk,’ so not much of an interesting origin to be found there either.

Chey’s job as well is quite similar as well. As a ICT Infrastructure engineer, he still has to work in a team environment, and has to apply technical skills to design, build, manage, and support the infrastructure service they provide. Fei’s appears to be the biggest outlier, whereas he holds the same general responsibility of everyone else (as listed under Chey; designing, building, managing, and supporting), their job as an IT engineer, depending on the placement, can either have them being the sole engineer in their environment, or part of a greater team. The main differences between the individual occupations is the environment, but otherwise, they’re actually rather similar; design something to help the company, ‘build’ it (in some contexts, this is just design but with extra steps), managing your team and those underneath you, as well as resources, data, etc., and supporting your company through your efforts and developments.
